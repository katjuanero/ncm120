IV. Interview Preparedness


Probable Interview Question Responses


My journey into healthcare began at a young age, inspired by my grandmother, who worked as a midwife. Watching her care for others with patience and dedication planted in me a deep respect for the profession and a dream to enter the world of healthcare.

Before becoming a nurse, my experiences as a student journalist and student leader helped me develop strong communication, empathy, and leadership skills. Those experiences shaped my passion for service and advocacy—values that continue to guide my practice today.

As a nurse, I take pride in providing safe, evidence-based, and compassionate care to every patient I encounter. I am committed to continuous learning and professional growth, aiming to become a nurse who not only heals but also inspires others through compassion, competence, and integrity.

My greatest strengths as a nurse are compassion, adaptability, and strong critical-thinking skills, ensuring that the care I provide goes beyond physical needs. Having worked in both Medical-Surgical and Emergency settings, I’ve developed the ability to stay calm and focused even under pressure. I also take pride in being a good communicator, which allows me to coordinate effectively with colleagues and provide holistic, patient-centered care.

One of my weaknesses is that I sometimes tend to take on too many responsibilities at once, especially when I want to ensure that all my patients receive the best care possible. I’ve learned, however, that nursing is a team effort, and that delegating tasks appropriately not only maintains efficiency but also improves patient safety.

I admire St. Luke’s for its commitment to excellence, continuous education, and compassionate care. Working in your institution would allow me to grow professionally while contributing to a culture that values quality and integrity in healthcare.
Applicant Questions
1. What training and development programs does St. Luke's Medical Center offer for staff nurses?


2. How does the institution support nurses who aspire to pursue specialization or leadership roles?


3. What are the key expectations for new nurses during the first six months of employment?
